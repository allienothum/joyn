description

The Interactive Design Capstone course at Maryville University challenges senior students to develop and produce a significant visual communication design project that culminates the skills, knowledge and understanding acquired throughout the program. The resulting project is expected to be multifaceted, intelligent, innovative, and purposeful.

Project Statement
JOYN is a multi­-platform application created to train the mind to become more positive. By utilizing science­-based training exercises and promoting social interactions, JOYN helps users create a more fulfilling and joyful life.

behind the idea

Negativity
    •   the expression of criticism or pessimism about something
    •   diminishes productivity, creativity, and energy
    •   causes excessive stress

Our Advantage
Psycologists have determined that up to 40% of happiness is the result of personal outlook and actions.

Positive Psychology
    •   the scientific study of what makes life most worth living
    •   our external world is not predictive of our happiness
    •   study what works, not what is broken
    •   the mind can be trained to become instictively positive

primary research

Questionnaire
23 couples / 0–3 children / married or living together
    •   78% — both work full-time outside of the home
    •   56% — spend 2 or less hours interacting alone daily (without technology)
    •   70% — in the past 12 months, have read a book, blog, or article together
    •   78% — set relationship goals together
    •   56% — use a specific app together
    •   65% — say their partner's mood significantly affects their own
    •   78% — desire more quality time with their partner
